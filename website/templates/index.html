About CipherDB

The CIPHER database uses a novel molecular nomenclature (CIPHER) to encode large amounts of information associated with any molecule in useable machine-readable form to enhance efficiencies in storage, speed, hierarchical extendibility, and software interoperability (Component 1), and an open access electronic synthetic chemistry portal (eSCP) and predictive chemistry software suite for the deposition, retrieval, and utilization of organic reaction information (Component 2) as inputs to an open source machine learning algorithm for the generative interactome-scale design of potent analgesic non-addictive drug-like small molecules (Component 3) that are validated using an automated high-speed system for analysis, synthesis, and bioassays targeting multiple key receptors (targets) implicated in both pain and addiction (Component 4). The entire system will be calibrated and verified using secondary in vivo assays of pain and addiction, aided by machine learning, to ensure that it can produce effective non-addictive analgesics without the need for further in vivo studies.
